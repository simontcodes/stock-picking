# Stock Investing App – Planning Document

## 1. Product Vision

**Purpose:**
Define the core goal of the app.

**Example:**
This app helps retail investors identify and evaluate high-quality stocks using a transparent scoring system based on fundamentals and growth metrics.

---

## 2. Target User

**Questions to answer:**

* Who is the user?
* What is their experience level?
* What problems do they face?

**Example:**

* Retail investors (age 20–45)
* Intermediate level (not beginners, not professionals)
* Struggle with:

  * Knowing which stocks to pick
  * Understanding financial data
  * Comparing companies quickly

---

## 3. Investing Philosophy

**Questions to answer:**

* What type of investing does the app support?
* What defines a “good stock”?

**Example:**
Hybrid approach:

* Growth (revenue growth, earnings growth)
* Quality (profit margins, ROE)
* Valuation (reasonable P/E, FCF yield)

A “good stock” is:

* Growing revenue consistently
* Profitable with strong margins
* Not overvalued

---

## 4. Core User Flows

**List what users will actually do in the app.**

**Example:**

* Sign up / log in
* Search for a stock (e.g., AAPL)
* View stock profile
* Analyze metrics
* Add to watchlist
* Compare with another stock
* View personal portfolio
* Rate or score a stock

---

## 9. Scoring System

**Define how stocks are evaluated.**

**Example Model:**

* Revenue growth → 20%
* Profitability → 20%
* Valuation → 20%
* Balance sheet strength → 20%
* Momentum → 20%

**Output:**
Each stock gets a score from 0–100.
